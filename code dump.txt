1. The Core Setup: The TB Architecture
Forget sub-funds and Net Asset Value (NAV). The Section 110 DAC operates on a single corporate ledger where the investor’s capital is treated as Debt, not Equity.
	•	The Bucket: The DAC is a single legal shell.
	•	The Assets: The European syndicated loans sit on the balance sheet as Assets.
	•	The Liability (The "Capstock"): The Australian Trust’s investment is recorded as the PPN Principal (a Liability).
	•	The Equity: Almost non-existent. Usually just €100 of initial share capital held by an orphan trust, plus the statutory €1,000/year retained profit.
2. The Operational Flow: Capital & Cash
Unlike a UCITS fund that processes daily subscriptions and redemptions (Trade Date accounting), this structure moves strictly on KKR’s instructions and Settlement Date accounting.
	•	The Drawdown (Subscription): KKR issues a "Drawdown Notice" to the Australian Trust. Your team does not touch the ledger yet.
	•	Cash Lands: Only when the cash hits the DAC’s bank account do you book the entry: Debit Cash / Credit PPN Principal.
	•	Funding the Loan: KKR trades a loan. Your Treasury team wires the cash to the seller. You book: Debit Loan Asset / Credit Cash.
	•	Cost Relief: When loans pay down or are sold, you must mirror KKR's system—typically using Specific Lot Identification or Weighted Average Cost (never LIFO, rarely FIFO).
3. The Month-End Flow: The "Profit Sweep"
This replaces your UCITS NAV strike. The goal of the DAC is to be tax-neutral, meaning it must legally sweep all excess profit out of the P&L and into the PPN Liability account.
The Workflow:
	1	Gather Data: Pull vendor prices (Mid/Bid), reconcile to KKR’s Loan Tape (for PIK and paydowns), and get the IFRS 9 Impairment (ECL) figures from KKR.
	2	Accrue Expenses: Book the Administrator, Audit, and KKR Management fees.
	3	Calculate the Sweep: * (Gross Income + Gains) - (Expenses + Impairments) - €83.33 (Monthly Tax Retention) = Available Profit.
	4	The "Zero Floor": If loans crash and the Available Profit is negative, the Sweep is exactly €0.00. You cannot book negative interest.
4. The Trial Balance (TB) Transformation
Here is exactly how your TB moves at month-end when you execute the flow above.
Step A: Pre-Sweep TB (Mid-Month) Your loans generated €500k in interest. Your expenses are €50k. Your P&L currently shows a massive €450k net profit. If left here, the DAC faces a huge tax bill.
Step B: The Sweep Journal Entry Your team calculates the sweep (€450,000 - €83 retained = €449,917). You manually book:
	•	Debit: PPN Interest Expense (P&L) for €449,917
	•	Credit: PPN Interest Payable (Liability) for €449,917
Step C: Post-Sweep TB (Month-End Final) The profit has been successfully "swept" onto the balance sheet.
