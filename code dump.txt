Scope (4 sentences)
This initiative deploys AI across the new business onboarding lifecycle — from RFP response through reverse due diligence, client workshop, and the production of a single-source Operating Memorandum — to eliminate redundant information gathering and accelerate time-to-live. The Operating Memorandum acts as a digital constitution for each client relationship, feeding directly into SLA construction and effort-based pricing so that commitments are consistent, traceable, and grounded in verified data. AI agents extract, reconcile, and structure client data at each stage, ensuring that project managers, operations, and relationship teams draw from one authoritative source rather than re-requesting the same information across handoffs. The result is a faster, higher-quality client experience with lower onboarding cost and reduced operational risk from day one.

Risk Areas Identified
Across the RFP-to-Operating-Memorandum flow, these are the key risk zones AI directly mitigates:

Information fragmentation — Client data today lives across emails, RFP responses, due diligence questionnaires, and workshop notes. Each handoff loses context and creates re-work. AI risk reduction: a single extraction-and-reconciliation layer builds the Operating Memorandum progressively, so nothing is asked twice.
Inconsistent SLA and pricing commitments — When the Operating Memorandum doesn't exist as a single source, SLA terms and pricing assumptions can diverge from what was actually discussed in workshops or due diligence. AI risk reduction: SLA and pricing parameters are derived directly from the verified Operating Memorandum, not from memory or scattered notes.
Reverse due diligence blind spots — Manual review of a prospect's fund structures, regulatory status, and operational complexity is time-intensive and prone to gaps. AI risk reduction: structured extraction from public filings, regulatory databases, and client-submitted documents surfaces risks and complexity drivers systematically.
Onboarding cycle time — Every week of delay between RFP win and go-live is a week of unrealised revenue and a deteriorating client first impression. AI risk reduction: parallel processing of documents and auto-population of downstream templates (SLA, pricing model) compresses the timeline.
Knowledge loss between teams — Project managers, operations leads, and relationship managers each re-discover what was already known at earlier stages. AI risk reduction: the Operating Memorandum is the single handoff artifact — every downstream consumer reads from it, not from the person who came before them.


Concrete Use Case: AI-Assisted Operating Memorandum Generation for a New AIF Mandate
Scenario: Your team wins an RFP to service a new Irish QIAIF with three sub-funds, complex NAV requirements, and multiple prime broker relationships.
Stage 1 — RFP Response: The AI agent ingests the client's RFP and your firm's response, extracting all commitments, service descriptions, and pricing assumptions into a structured data layer. This becomes the seed of the Operating Memorandum.
Stage 2 — Reverse Due Diligence: The AI agent pulls the client's CBI filings, prospectus, and any publicly available fund documentation. It flags complexity drivers (multi-prime, side pockets, performance fee crystallisation) and pre-populates the due diligence section of the Operating Memorandum with verified data and open questions for the client.
Stage 3 — Workshop: During the client workshop, the AI agent captures decisions and requirements in real time (from structured notes or transcript), reconciles them against what was already in the Operating Memorandum, and highlights any contradictions with RFP commitments or due diligence findings.
Stage 4 — Operating Memorandum Published: The consolidated Operating Memorandum is produced — a single document covering fund structure, service scope, operational requirements, regulatory obligations, key contacts, and open items. It is version-controlled and becomes the digital constitution for the relationship.
Stage 5 — SLA and Pricing Derived: The AI agent reads the Operating Memorandum and auto-generates a draft SLA (mapping service commitments to measurable KPIs) and feeds complexity drivers into your effort-based pricing model to produce a defensible fee proposal. Project managers onboarding the fund never need to ask "what did we agree?" — it's all in the memorandum.
Measurable quick-win targets: 50% reduction in onboarding elapsed time, zero duplicate information requests to the client, and SLA/pricing alignment verified before go-live rather than discovered after.
